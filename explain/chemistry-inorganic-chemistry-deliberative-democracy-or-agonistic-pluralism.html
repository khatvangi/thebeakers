The Challenge

Current democratic models often reduce politics to rational consensus, ignoring the role of passions and conflicts. Deliberative democracy, while richer than aggregative models, still struggles to account for the inherent tensions in democratic politics. This research questions whether democracy must reconcile liberalism with conflict or suppress it.
