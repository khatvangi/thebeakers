Violacein Targets Bladder Cancer Cells

Can a natural pigment fight bladder cancer cells?

Oncology Letters

Scene 1

The Challenge

Bladder cancer remains a leading urological concern with limited treatment options. Current therapies like surgery, chemotherapy, and immunotherapy have gaps, prompting research into natural compounds like violacein for novel anti-cancer strategies.

The Hypothesis

Researchers hypothesized that violacein's known anti-proliferative effects on other cancers might also target bladder cancer cells, particularly high-grade transitional cell carcinoma models.

The Method

Scientists tested violacein on T24 (low-grade) and 5637 (high-grade) bladder cancer cells, measuring viability, proliferation, cell cycle changes, and caspase-3 activation to determine its mechanism of action.

The Discovery

Violacein significantly reduced proliferation in both cell lines, with stronger effects on 5637 cells. It induced cell cycle arrest and caspase-3 activation, but via distinct pathways in each cell line.
