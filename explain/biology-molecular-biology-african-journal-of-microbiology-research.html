The Challenge

Low rhizobia efficiency limits legume crop yields. Limited genetic diversity hampers strain improvement efforts.

The Method

Researchers isolated rhizobia from peanut nodules using yeast mannitol agar and Box-PCR. Selected isolates were tested for phosphate solubilization, salt tolerance, and symbiotic efficiency in non-autoclaved soils.

The Discovery

Most isolates acidified cultures and showed rapid growth. Genetic analysis revealed high variability, with two strains showing potential for enhanced symbiotic performance.
