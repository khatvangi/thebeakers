The Challenge

This study addresses how genetic mutations in MECP2 disrupt neural circuits linked to social behavior and anxiety. It solves the problem of understanding the mechanistic link between Rett syndrome and amygdala dysfunction.

The Method

Researchers used conditional knockout mice and ex vivo electrophysiology to analyze amygdala circuits. In vivo functional ultrasound imaging tracked connectivity between amygdala, hippocampus, and prefrontal cortex.

The Discovery

Mecp2 loss reduced GABAergic input to amygdala neurons and weakened functional connectivity with key brain regions. Excitability of principal neurons increased in adult animals.
