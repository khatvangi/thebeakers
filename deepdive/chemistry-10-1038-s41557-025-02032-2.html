**Bringing Biocatalysis into Teaching Labs: A New Frontier in Chemical Education**

**Abstract:** As catalysts have become a staple of university chemistry courses, biocatalysis â€“ the use of enzymes to facilitate chemical reactions â€“ has only recently been integrated into curricula despite its vast applications in industry. This article explores the benefits and practicalities of incorporating biocatalysis into undergraduate teaching labs.

**1. ONE-SENTENCE CLAIM:** Biocatalysis offers a novel approach to chemical synthesis, providing a sustainable alternative to traditional methods by utilizing enzymes to catalyze reactions with minimal waste and energy consumption.

**2. WHY IT MATTERS (2-3 sentences):** The increasing demand for eco-friendly practices in industries such as pharmaceuticals, agriculture, and biofuels has made biocatalysis an attractive alternative to traditional synthetic chemistry approaches. By introducing biocatalysis into teaching labs, students can gain hands-on experience with a versatile tool that has the potential to revolutionize chemical synthesis. This approach also aligns with current pedagogical trends emphasizing experiential learning and real-world applications.

**3. THE MECHANISM (3 steps: A â†’ B â†’ C):**

A. **Enzyme binding**: The enzyme substrate complex forms, where the enzyme binds to a specific substrate molecule, positioning it for optimal reaction conditions.

B. **Catalytic conversion**: The enzyme facilitates a chemical transformation by lowering the activation energy required for the reaction, allowing the substrate to convert into a product.

C. **Protein turnover**: After completing the catalytic cycle, the enzyme is released from the product and can reform a new substrate complex, repeating the process.

**4. CURRICULUM BRIDGE (connect to undergrad concepts):**

Biocatalysis builds upon fundamental undergraduate concepts in kinetics and diffusion. Students familiar with these topics will appreciate how enzymes interact with substrates to facilitate reactions. Additionally, understanding enzyme kinetics and mechanisms can provide insight into other biochemical processes, such as metabolism and detoxification.

**5. WHY TO DOUBT IT (one limitation):**

One potential limitation of biocatalysis is the requirement for specific conditions, such as temperature and pH, which may not be easily replicable in all teaching labs. This can limit the scope of experiments that can be performed under these constraints.

**6. TAKEAWAY:** Biocatalysis offers a unique opportunity for undergraduate students to explore an eco-friendly approach to chemical synthesis while developing hands-on experience with enzyme kinetics and mechanisms. By incorporating biocatalytic techniques into their curriculum, educators can provide students with practical skills and knowledge that align with current industry needs and trends in sustainable chemistry.
