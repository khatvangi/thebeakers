On vibration suppression of a tendon-driven soft robotic neck for the social robot HARU

The Vibration Problem

The soft robotic neck has an unactuated yaw axis that vibrates when carrying loads. This happens because the tendon-driven system lacks direct control over this axis, causing unwanted oscillations during movement.

Scene 2

CIVS Solution

CIVS uses feedback from a sensor to detect yaw vibrations. It modulates tension in the actuated tendons to dampen the unactuated motion, without adding extra hardware. This creates a 'virtual damper' through mechanical coupling.

flowchart LR A[Yaw Acceleration Sensor] --> B[High-Pass Filter] B --> C[Tendon Tension Modulation] C --> D[Increased Damping Without Hardware]
Scene 3

Control System Combo

The system combines three parts: PID control for pitch/roll, current-based pretensioning for stability, and CIVS for yaw. This synergy ensures smooth, compliant motion while suppressing vibrations effectively.

Benchmark Test

Sliding mode control was tested as a nonlinear alternative, but CIVS performed better. It reduced yaw vibrations more than PID or sliding mode, proving indirect feedback is more effective for this underactuated system.
