Evolution of Legged Robots

1984-86

Raibert's Leg Lab - First dynamic hoppers proved simple feedback enables locomotion

2005-08

BigDog - Hydraulic quadruped shows military-grade terrain traversal

2014

MIT Cheetah - Proprioceptive actuators revolutionize electric quadrupeds

2018-19

DRL Breakthrough - Sim-to-real transfer works! ANYmal, Unitree democratize access

2023-24

Humanoid Explosion - Bipeds learn parkour, soccer, manipulation. New era begins.

Control Approaches Compared

Reinforcement Learning

  • Fast inference (neural network forward pass)
  • Handles model uncertainty via randomization
  • Direct sensor → action mapping
  • Implicit state estimation in policy
  • Can incorporate vision directly

Model Predictive Control

  • Explicit physics model integration
  • Constraint satisfaction guarantees
  • Interpretable planning horizon
  • Transfer knowledge across tasks
  • Safety-critical applications

Bridging Sim-to-Real Gap

Domain Randomization

Randomize mass, friction, latency during training

Privileged Learning

Teacher with full info → Student with sensors

System ID

Learn actuator dynamics from real data

Domain Adaptation

Identify environment at runtime, adapt policy

Open Challenges

Safety Guarantees

How do we ensure robots never harm humans? Constrained RL and safety filters need more work.

Loco-Manipulation

Walking while carrying and manipulating objects remains extremely challenging.

Skill Transfer

Policies don't generalize well. Each skill needs separate reward engineering.

Foundation Models

Can LLMs/VLMs help with planning and reasoning for robots? Early days.

Key Takeaways

1

PPO dominates locomotion learning due to stable convergence and adaptability

2

Privileged learning is essential: teacher with perfect info trains student with real sensors

3

Domain randomization makes sim-to-real work by training for every possible variation

4

Affordable hardware (Unitree A1/Go1) democratized the field for researchers

5

Humanoids are next: lessons from quadrupeds now applying to bipedal robots

6

Ethics matter: researchers must engage with weaponization and job displacement concerns
